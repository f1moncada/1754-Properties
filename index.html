The CFO Lens — What to Pressure-Test

This is the sponsor's underwriting. Before committing equity, a disciplined investor would validate the following. Green items already lean conservative; amber items are where the return is most exposed.

Exit cap compression is assumed

Underwriting exits at 8.0% vs. an 8.9% entry — ~90 bps of compression. Defensible given a high entry basis, but at a flat 8.9% exit the IRR falls into the low-to-mid 20s. Test the slider above.

Floating-rate exposure

Debt priced at SOFR + 3.75%. The high dividend yields imply interest-only / minimal amortization. Confirm rate cap, hedge, and amortization terms — the cash-on-cash is sensitive to the SOFR path.

ADR index held flat at 88%

The model never closes the gap to the competitive set. That is conservative — but also means the 5.6% ADR CAGR rides on overall market growth, not penetration gains.

Internal ratio consistency

The stated DSCR row (1.6×–1.9×) and the dividend-yield row imply slightly different debt-service figures. Tie the levered cash flow to a single debt schedule before relying on the 13.2% Yr-1 yield.

Occupancy assumption is conservative

Proforma holds 90.4% vs. 92.2% (2025A) and 91.1% (TTM). Modeling below recent actuals builds in cushion.

Cost structure is stress-tested

Wages +8%/yr and room expense +30% over the hold, yet NOI margin still holds ~42% and the deal clears 33% IRR. Downside union-wage case still returns ~18% IRR per the sponsor.
